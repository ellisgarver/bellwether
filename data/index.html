where the words come from

the source set is a fixed basis spanning every independent dimension of U.S. macro discourse, including the upstream writing of policymakers, researchers, and analysts. eight discourse dimensions plus a cross-cutting Q&A register, across twelve active source feeds, each carrying several document types (articles, speeches, reports). open a row for the underlying series:

01 U.S. monetary authority +

Federal Reserve Board: FOMC statements and minutes, speeches (including Jackson Hole), the Beige Book, FEDS Notes, the Monetary Policy Report, and the Financial Stability Report.

02 U.S. monetary research +

the regional Federal Reserve banks: New York (Liberty Street Economics, Staff Reports), San Francisco (Economic Letter, Working Papers, FedViews, Beige Book, Community Development), Chicago (research series, the president's speeches, business and economic-conditions surveys), and Atlanta (macroblog, working papers).

03 international macro authority +

the IMF: the World Economic Outlook, the Global Financial Stability Report, Finance & Development, working papers, and the IMF blog.

04 international central-bank network +

the BIS: the Quarterly Review, working papers, bulletins, and a curated set of central-bank speeches.

05 U.S. fiscal authority +

the CBO (cost estimates, the Budget and Economic Outlook, working papers, the Monthly Budget Review) and the Council of Economic Advisers (the Economic Report of the President).

06 U.S. financial-stability research +

the Treasury's Office of Financial Research: working papers and FSOC reports.

07 U.S. policy think tanks +

Brookings (Economic Studies and the Hutchins Center) and the Peterson Institute for International Economics.

08 academic work and commentary +

the NBER working-paper series and VoxEU, the CEPR's policy-column portal.

09 cross-cutting Q&A register +

Congressional testimony from the Treasury Secretary.

every article from every source enters the corpus; nothing is filtered by topic on the way in. the only ingest filters are the date window (2010 onward) and near-duplicate removal: MinHash over character 5-grams, dropping documents above 0.85 Jaccard similarity (Broder 1997; Henzinger 2006), keyed on both article URL and content. within-macro topical scope is decided later, in clustering.

the financial press (WSJ, Bloomberg, FT, etc) sits downstream of these sources, tracked separately by volume overlay.
